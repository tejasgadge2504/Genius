JEE Mains and Adv

Introduction: The Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) is a highly prestigious examination for students aspiring to pursue undergraduate programs in engineering across India. To facilitate a seamless application process and ensure eligibility, candidates must adhere to specific criteria set by the examination authorities. This comprehensive guide provides an overview of the JEE 2024 eligibility criteria, encompassing crucial details about the examination.
JEE 2024 Overview:
- Full Name: Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) 2024
- Conducting Body: National Testing Agency (NTA)
- Frequency: Conducted twice a year (January and April sessions)
- Exam Level: National Level
- Languages: English, Hindi, and other regional languages
- Application Form Mode: Online
- Mode of Exam: Computer-Based Test (CBT)
- Participating Institutes: IITs, NITs, IIITs, and other premier engineering institutes across India
Seat Allocation: - JEE is the gateway for admissions to prestigious institutes like IITs, NITs, IIITs, and other top engineering colleges across India.

Eligibility Criteria :
1. Nationality: - Indian nationals and foreign nationals are eligible for JEE 2024.
2. Age Limitations: - There is no fixed age limit for candidates appearing in JEE 2024.
3. Qualification: - Candidates must have passed the 10+2 examination or equivalent from a recognized board or university.
- Required Courses: Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.
4. Year of Qualification: - Candidates who have appeared or will appear in the 10+2 examination in the same year as the JEE examination are eligible.

NEET

Introduction: The National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET) is a critical examination for students aspiring to pursue undergraduate programs in medicine and dental sciences in India. To facilitate a seamless application process and ensure eligibility, candidates must adhere to specific criteria set by the examination authorities. This comprehensive guide provides an overview of the NEET 2024 eligibility criteria, encompassing crucial details about the examination.
NEET 2024 Overview:
- Full Name: National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET) 2024
- Conducting Body: National Testing Agency (NTA)
- Frequency: Conducted once a year
- Exam Level: National Level
- Languages: English, Hindi, and other regional languages
- Application Form Mode: Online
- Mode of Exam: Pen and Paper (Offline)
- Participating Institutes: Medical and dental colleges across India, including AIIMS and JIPMER
Seat Allocation: - NEET is the gateway for admissions to medical and dental colleges, including prestigious institutions like AIIMS and JIPMER.

Eligibility Criteria :
1. Nationality: - Indian nationals, Non-Resident Indians (NRIs), Overseas Citizens of India (OCIs), Persons with Indian Origin (PIOs), and foreign nationals are eligible for NEET.
2. Age Limitations: - The minimum age for candidates is 17 years as of December 31 of the admission year. - There is no upper age limit for NEET.
3. Qualification: - Candidates must have passed the 10+2 examination or equivalent from a recognized board or university.
- Required Courses: Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Biotechnology, and English.
4. Minimum Aggregate Score: - General category applicants must have a minimum aggregate score of 50% in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology/Biotechnology.
- SC/ST/OBC category applicants must have a minimum score of 40%.

HSC Boards

Maharashtra State Board HSC (Class 12) Examinations:
1. Exam Conducting Authority: The Maharashtra State Board of Secondary and Higher Secondary Education (MSBSHSE) administers the HSC (Class 12) board exams annually.
2. Syllabus and Paper Pattern: MSBSHSE plays a pivotal role in designing the syllabus and determining the paper pattern for Class 12 examinations. Students are advised to follow this prescribed curriculum and familiarize themselves with the question paper format.
3. Preparation Strategy: To excel in Maha HSC exams, students are encouraged to solve previous years' question papers. This practice not only provides insight into the exam pattern but also aids in refining problem-solving skills.
4. Divisional Boards: The HSC and SSC Examinations are conducted across the state of Maharashtra through nine Divisional Boards situated in Pune, Mumbai, Aurangabad, Nasik, Kolhapur, Amravati, Latur, Nagpur, and Ratnagiri.
5. Passing Criteria: According to Maharashtra HSC Board's qualifying mandates, students must secure a minimum of 35% or above to pass the 12th board exams. This criterion encompasses both practical and theory exam scores.
6. Supplementary Exams: In the event that a student fails to achieve the minimum passing percentage, they are required to appear for Maharashtra HSC Supplementary exams. These supplementary exams offer a second chance for students to clear the HSC examinations.
